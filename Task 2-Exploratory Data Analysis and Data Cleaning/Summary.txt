The BCG project team believes that building a churn model to understand whether price sensitivity is the largest driver of customer churn has strong potential.

The client has provided the following datasets:

Historical customer data: Includes customer usage patterns, sign-up dates, and forecasted usage.

Historical pricing data: Contains variable and fixed pricing information over time.

Churn indicator: Flags whether a customer has churned or remained active.
The ojective is to Perform exploratory data analysis (EDA) using Python to:

Examine the data types of each column

Understand data distributions and basic descriptive statistics

Identify patterns, outliers, and trends that may relate to churn

Define and calculate a price sensitivity metric

Explore its potential correlation with customer churn
